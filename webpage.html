E-Waste Awareness Hub

What is E-Waste?

E-waste refers to discarded electronic devices such as mobile phones, computers, televisions, batteries, and chargers. When not disposed of properly, e-waste releases toxic chemicals that harm the environment and human health.

Why is E-Waste Dangerous?

  • Contains harmful substances like lead and mercury
  • Pollutes soil and groundwater
  • Affects human health and wildlife
  • Increases carbon footprint

How Can We Reduce E-Waste?

  • Repair electronics instead of replacing them
  • Donate or recycle old devices
  • Avoid unnecessary upgrades
  • Buy eco-friendly electronics

Your Role


As a responsible citizen, you can help reduce e-waste by spreading awareness, recycling properly, and choosing sustainable products. Small actions can make a big difference.

Valuables found in E-Waste

E-waste (electronic waste) contains many valuable materials that can be recovered and reused. The main valuable things found in e-waste are:

    Precious metals:
  • Gold, silver, and platinum used in circuit boards and connectors.

    • Base metals:
  • Copper, aluminum, iron, and steel found in wires, frames, and components.

    • Rare earth elements:
  • Neodymium, europium, yttrium, etc. used in magnets, screens, and speakers.

    • Plastics:
  • High-quality plastics from casings, keyboards, and cables.

    • Glass:
  • From screens and monitors.

    • Batteries materials:
  • Lithium, cobalt, nickel especially from mobile phone and laptop batteries.
These materials make e-waste valuable for recycling, and proper recycling helps save natural resources and reduce pollution.
